Himachal Pradesh has experienced severe landslides and floods, resulting in over 350 deaths, making it the worst-hit state in North India for 2024's natural disasters.

In Uttar Pradesh, heavy rainfall and subsequent flooding have devastated approximately 4 lakh hectares of farmland, causing widespread agricultural damage and impacting farmers' livelihoods.

Cyclonic disturbances in the Bay of Bengal have contributed to abnormal weather patterns in North India, exacerbating flood risks and causing significant disruption in states like Assam and Uttarakhand.

Uttarakhand continues to struggle with frequent flooding, damaging infrastructure and causing loss of life, placing additional pressure on disaster response teams in the region.
